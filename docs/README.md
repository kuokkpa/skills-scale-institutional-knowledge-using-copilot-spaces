# OctoAcme Process Documentation

This folder contains OctoAcme's program and process documents — covering project lifecycle guides, roles and personas, risk and communication standards, release procedures, and continuous improvement practices. These docs serve as the authoritative reference for how OctoAcme plans, executes, and improves cross-functional projects.

## OctoAcme Project Management Overview

OctoAcme follows a structured lifecycle from **Initiation** through to **Retrospective**. Projects begin with a lightweight one-pager that confirms the business need, success metrics, and stakeholder alignment before a go/no-go decision is made to proceed into planning. During **Planning**, work is broken into prioritized, shippable increments with clear acceptance criteria, a Definition of Done, a release milestone map, and a Risk Register capturing dependencies and mitigation strategies. See [`octoacme-project-initiation.md`](octoacme-project-initiation.md) and [`octoacme-project-planning.md`](octoacme-project-planning.md) for details.

**Execution and Tracking** rely on a project board (Backlog → Ready → In Progress → In Review → QA → Done), daily standups, and weekly delivery syncs to surface blockers early. Pull Requests are kept small (≤ 400 lines when possible), must link to an issue, and require passing CI (automated tests, linting, and security scans) plus at least one approval before merge. QA encompasses unit tests, integration tests, end-to-end smoke tests for critical flows, and manual acceptance testing where needed. After a successful verification in staging, releases are deployed through an automated pipeline with a documented rollback plan and post-deploy verification before announcing to stakeholders. See [`octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md`](octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md) and [`octoacme-release-and-deployment.md`](octoacme-release-and-deployment.md) for full checklists.

Key **roles** include the **Project Manager (PM)** who owns delivery coordination, schedules, and risk communications; the **Product Manager (PdM/Product Lead)** who defines outcomes and prioritizes the backlog; **Developers** who implement, test, and review code; **QA/Testing** who validate acceptance criteria; and **Stakeholders** who provide inputs and approvals. Communication follows a regular cadence — twice-weekly standups for the delivery team, a weekly PM + PdM sync, and monthly stakeholder updates — with a single source of truth (the project README or release doc) used for status reporting. Risks are tracked in the Risk Register, reviewed at weekly syncs, and escalated via a clear path (Team → PM → Product Lead → Sponsor) when needed. See [`octoacme-roles-and-personas.md`](octoacme-roles-and-personas.md) and [`octoacme-risks-and-communication.md`](octoacme-risks-and-communication.md) for guidance.

Each sprint, release, or milestone closes with a **Retrospective** that captures what went well, what could be improved, and 2–3 prioritized action items with owners and due dates. Action items are added to the backlog and tracked in the weekly PM sync, fostering a culture of small, measurable, iterative improvements. See [`octoacme-retrospective-and-continuous-improvement.md`](octoacme-retrospective-and-continuous-improvement.md) for the full structure and templates.
